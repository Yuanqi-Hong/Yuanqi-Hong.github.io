Graphing CITES Ivory Trading Data

BY YUANQI HONG

July 30, 2018

CITES is short for the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora. It is an international agreement between governments, aiming to ensure that international trade in specimens of wild animals and plants does not threaten their survival.
CITES maintains a trade database of various goods/objects. In this article we are going to look at a dataset of trade in ivory carvings.
In this dataset we have reported quantities of ivory carvings traded both by importers and exporters. Sometimes, quantities reported by each party differ; we take whichever is larger. Also, the units of the quantities are a bit messy: these include grams, kilograms, sets, etc.; sometimes the unit is simply missing. In the ensuing analysis, all units are converted to kilograms (kg), except for those that couldn't be converted, for instance sets, in which case they are assumed to be equal to 1 kg. For data missing a unit, we drop it. Thus, the chart you see below represents a picture that could only have a smaller scope than the reality of ivory trade.
